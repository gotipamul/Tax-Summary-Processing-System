INCOME

1) Income From Salary:- Income from salary is the sum of Basic salary+HRA+Special Allowance+Transport Allowance+any other Allowance. Some components of your salary are exempt from tax, such as telephone bills reimbursement, leave travel allowance.

2) Income From House:- Income From House property. Add any rental income, or include interest paid on home loan (use negative value for interest paid).

3) Other Income:- Income from other sources like saving account interest income,fixed deposit interest income, interest income from bonds.

DEDUCTIONS

1) Standard Deductions:- Standard Deduction allows for a flat deduction from a salary income, to make up for some of the expenses which an employee would typically incur in relation to his employment. Its 50,000 for FY 2020-21 (old regime) and FY 2019-20. No standard deduction can be claimed for FY 2020-21 New Regime.

2) Deduction u/s 80C:- The investment u/s 80C/80CCC that qualify for income tax detuction are: ~Term insurance policies ~Unit linked insurance plans ~Traditional Savings/Endowment Insurance Policies ~Retirement Insurance Plans ~5 year fixed deposits ~Public provident fund ~Equity linked savings scheme

3)Deduction u/s 80D:- The premiums paid towards health insurance policies taken for self,spouse,children or parents is eligible for tax deduction u/s80D of Income Tax ACT 1961, The maximum limit for deduction for health insurance policy premium taken for self , spouse or children is 25000 ( the maximum limit is increased to 50000 if the age of the person is insured is above 60 years. So,if person buys medical insurance for , self and parents who are senior citizen he/she will be allowed deduction upto 75,000(self 25,000+parents 50,000). Additional deduction upto 25,000 is allowed on health insurance premium paid towards covering parents (the max limit is increased to 50,000 if the age of the person insured is above 60 years . If person who is senior citizen buys medical insurance for self and parents who are senior citizen he/she will be allowed deduction upto 1,00,000(self 50,000+parents 50,000).

4) HRA Deductions:- HRA Deductions is applicable minimum of the following : If you live in a rented flat , you can claim tax deduction on the rent you pay. An amount equal to 50% of the basic salary (for accomodation in Mumbai ,kolkata,Delhi,or Chennai) and 40% of basic salary OR The actual amount of HRA received by the assesse in respect of the relevant period.

5) Deduction u/s 80CCD (1B):- Deduction for self -contribution to NPS-section 80CCD (1B). A new section 80CCD (1B) has been introduced for an additional deduction of up to Rs 50,000 for the amount deposited by a tax payer to their NPS account. Contributions to Atal Pension Yojana are also eligible. If you have invested in NPS , you can get additional deduction upto Rs 50,000 u/s 80CCD (1B) which will be over and above deduction of Rs 1,50,000 u/s 80C.
